  • The Structural Hierarchy:

    In our Union of Prayer, we will model our hierarchy after the knight’s ranks during the Medieval times. We will have three ranks building up to the Knights and Ladies ranking. For the Knights: Pages, Squires and ultimately Knights. For the Ladies: Maid, Ladies-in-Waiting, and ultimately Ladies. Every member entering the Union of Prayer will start at the lowest level and work towards the highest level. Each rank will have a minimum service requirement and be required to write a request of permission documenting why they would like to be elevated to the next rank. There is a minimum age requirement for each level; however, there is no time restraint on how long a member can be at one particular rank.
  • Pages and Maids:

    This is the introduction rank. Boys and men will be called Pages; girls and women will be designated as Maids. All members will be started at this rank regardless of age. Each member must complete 40 hours of service to the community before advancing to the next rank. (The 40 hours reminds us of the 40 days and nights Jesus spent in desert before the start of ministry.) Once these 40 hours have been completed and documented, the Page or Maid may submit a written petition to advance to the next rank. The Page or Maid must be at least 13 in order to advance to the next rank. After reviewing the written request, the Page or Maid will be called forth for an interview. During the interview, the Page and Maid will be required to recite the Knights and Ladies of Chivalry Code of Conduct, the mission statement, and their promise to defend life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. During the ranking ceremony, they will be again asked to recite the code, mission statement, and their promise. In addition they would also present to the ceremonial audience a description and report of the service project that made the most impact on their life. At the completion of the ceremony, they would be advanced to the next rank as a Squire or Lady-in-Waiting.
  • Squires and Ladies-in-Waiting:

    As a middle ranking member the young men and men will be called Squires; young ladies and women will be designated as Ladies-in-Waiting. A member must be at least 13 before reaching this rank. Each member must complete 70 hours of service to the community before advancing to the next rank. (The 70 hours reminds us of the 70 disciples Jesus sent into the surrounding areas as his witnesses.) Once these 70 hours have been completed and documented, the Squire or Lady-in-Waiting may submit a written petition and fill out the application to advance to the next rank. The Squire or Lady-in-Waiting must be at least 18 in order to advance to the next rank. After reviewing the written request and application, the Squire or Lady-in-Waiting will be called forth for an interview. During the interview, the Squire and Lady-in-Waiting will be required answer a series of questions in order to determine that he/she will abide by the code of conduct and agrees with the statutes in regards to life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. They will also be called upon to recite the Knights and Ladies of Chivalry Code of Conduct, the mission statement, and their promise to defend life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. During the ranking ceremony, they will be again asked to recite the code, mission statement, and their promise. In addition, they will be required to recite the prayer for protection of life, the prayer for families, the prayer for marriage, and the prayer for religious freedom. They will also have to describe the coat of arms and the symbolization of each item from memory. Finally they would give a presentation to the ceremonial audience on the service project or event that made the most impact on their life and the lives of the community. At the completion of the ceremony, they would be advanced to the next rank as a Knight or Lady.
  • Knights and Ladies:

    This is the introduction rank. Boys and men will be called Pages; girls and women will be designated as Maids. All members will be started at this rank regardless of age. Each member must complete 40 hours of service to the community before advancing to the next rank. (The 40 hours reminds us of the 40 days and nights Jesus spent in desert before the start of ministry.) Once these 40 hours have been completed and documented, the Page or Maid may submit a written petition to advance to the next rank. The Page or Maid must be at least 13 in order to advance to the next rank. After reviewing the written request, the Page or Maid will be called forth for an interview. During the interview, the Page and Maid will be required to recite the Knights and Ladies of Chivalry Code of Conduct, the mission statement, and their promise to defend life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. During the ranking ceremony, they will be again asked to recite the code, mission statement, and their promise. In addition they would also present to the ceremonial audience a description and report of the service project that made the most impact on their life. At the completion of the ceremony, they would be advanced to the next rank as a Squire or Lady-in-Waiting.
  • Veritatis et Gaudium Knight or Lady Status:

    As the highest ranking title member the men will be called Knights and the women will be designated as Ladies. A member must be at least 18 before reaching this rank. Although this is the highest title someone may achieve, there are various ranks that a Knight or Lady may reach within this title. Veritatis et Veritate Knight or Lady Status: To achieve the Veritatis et Veritate rank, the Knight or Lady must demonstrate truth and loyalty. The loyalty of a Knight or Lady will be measured by the Knight’s or Lady’s loyalty to the Union of Prayer. Loyalty will be displayed by regularly attending meetings, organizing events, serving the community, and attending events. A 75% attendance rate will be required to meet the status of Veritatis et Veritate for a minimum of 18 months. The Knight or Lady must show supportiveness, dignity, honesty, and sincerity in living out the Code of Conduct, mission statement, and promise to support life, marriage families, and religious freedom. To achieve this rank a minimum of 89 service hours must be completed (89 reminds of the 89 books that make up the four gospels of Good News). The Knight or Lady must submit a written request to advance to the next level. After reviewing the written request, the Knight or Lady will be called forth for an interview. During the interview, the Knight and Lady will be required to answer questions related to truth and loyalty as well as recite the Knights and Ladies of Chivalry Code of Conduct, the mission statement, and their promise to defend life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. During the ranking and color ceremony will they will be clothed in blue representing truth and loyalty, and they will be again asked to recite the code, mission statement, and their promise. In addition, they will be required to recite the prayer for protection of life, the prayer for families, the prayer for marriage, and the prayer for religious freedom. They will also have to describe the coat of arms and the symbolization of each item from memory. Finally they will give a speech on loyalty and truth. At the completion of the ceremony, they would be awarded the rank of Veritatis et Veritate which stands for Truth and Loyalty.
  • Iustitia Knight or Lady Status:

    To achieve the Iustitia rank, the Knight or Lady must demonstrate justice. The Knight/ Lady will be measured by the justice they display. A Knight or Lady’s justice will be measure by his/her activeness in defending life, marriage, and religious freedom. He/she would demonstrate justice by showing a concern for justice, peace and genuine respect for all members of the Union of Prayer as well as the community. This Knight or Lady in preparation of achieving this rank will have been placed in charge of teaching the Squires/Ladies-in-Waiting the Code of Conduct and what it means and how to defend life, marriage, families, and religious freedom and how they can do that in their daily lives as well as training them to lead events. The Knight/Lady will also need to have written a letter to our political figures requesting that they too defend life, marriage, and religious freedom at some point during the course of their being a Knight or Lady. He/She will also have led/hosted/directed at least three events in each of the following areas life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. The Knight or Lady will also have showed justice while serving for at least for one year on the council of interviewers. To achieve this rank a minimum of 316 service hours must be completed (316 reminds us of John 3:16 which states that God loved the world so much that he gave his only Son that whoever believes in Him shall have everlasting life). The Knight or Lady must submit a written request to advance to the next level. After reviewing the written request, the Knight or Lady will be called forth for an interview. During the interview, the Knight and Lady will be required to answer questions related to justice as well as recite the Knights and Ladies of Chivalry Code of Conduct, the mission statement, and their promise to defend life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. During the ranking and color ceremony will they will be clothed in purple representing justice, and they will be again asked to recite the code, mission statement, and their promise. In addition, they will be required to recite the prayer for protection of life, the prayer for families, the prayer for marriage, and the prayer for religious freedom. They will also have to describe the coat of arms and the symbolization of each item from memory. Finally they will give a talk on how we fight for justice. At the completion of the ceremony, they would be awarded the rank of Iustitia which stands for Justice.
  • Victoriae et Quoad Knight or Lady Status:

    To achieve the Victoriae et Quoad rank, the Knight or Lady must be victorious and earned respect. A Knight or Lady will have been victorious and earned respect by his/her activeness in fighting for life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. This Knight or Lady in preparation of achieving this rank will have been placed in charge of teaching the Knights and Ladies and holding them accountable for following the Code of Conduct, defending life, supporting marriage, assisting families, and promoting religious freedom. He/She will also have led/hosted/directed at least seven events in each of the following areas life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. To achieve this rank a minimum of 490 service hours must be completed (490 reminds us that Christ told us that we must forgive one another 7x70). The Knight or Lady must submit a written request to advance to the next level. After reviewing the written request, the Knight or Lady will be called forth for an interview. During the interview, the achievements of the Knight or Lady will be read aloud before the council of interviewers by the Grand Knight or Lady and it will be recommended that this Knight or Lady be awarded the final and highest rank, Victoriae et Quoad. The Knight and Lady will be asked to recite the Knights and Ladies of Chivalry Code of Conduct, the mission statement, and their promise to defend life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. They will be again asked to recite the code, mission statement, and their promise. In addition, they will be required to recite the prayer for protection of life, the prayer for families, the prayer for marriage, and the prayer for religious freedom. They will also have to describe the coat of arms and the symbolization of each item from memory. The list of lifetime achievements will be read once more before the members of the Union of Prayer. A Page/Maid and a Squire/Lady and a Knight/Lady of each rank will be asked to give a statement of honor showing respect for the member receiving the rank. The Grand Knight or Lady will then speak and afterwards clothed the Knight or Lady in maroon representing victory and respect. Finally the Knight/Lady will share of their experience as a member of the Knights and Ladies of Chivalry Union of Prayer while emphasizing how we can be victorious and respect those around us, but especially life, marriage, families, and religious freedom. At the completion of the ceremony, he/she would be awarded the highest rank, Victoriae et Quoad, which stands for Victorious and Respect.

About

Who We Are ?

We are the Knights and Ladies of Chivalry, a Union of Prayer. Our inspiration comes from the knights of old who lived by a code of honor and the needs of our society today. By integrating the two, we have developed a Union of Prayer dedicated to living our lives in outstanding conduct in our families, communities, state, and country. We will also be committed to praying for the protection of life in all its stages, the safeguard of marriage, the fortification of families, and the preservation of religious freedom. The Knights and Ladies of Chivalry will follow the same code of conduct used by the knights while serving families and communities, sharing the love of Jesus, and loving those around us.

Code Of Chivalry

The "Code of Chivalry" is the same code that was originally used by the Medieval knights. We have faith that if The Code worked for them, then it is a good start for us as well! So here it is:

To fear God and maintain His Church
To serve the liege Lord in valor and faith
To protect the weak and defenseless
To give succor to widows and orphans
To refrain from the wanton giving of offence
To live by honor but not for glory
To despise pecuniary reward
To fight for the welfare of all
To obey those placed in authority
To guard the honor of fellow knights and ladies
To eschew unfairness, meanness and deceit
To keep faith
To speak the truth at all times
To persevere to the end in any enterprise begun
To respect the honor of all people
